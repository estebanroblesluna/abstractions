Project's brief
The Casino team develops games and technology for ID Interactive, a gaming company located in Florida. Based on a concept and artwork from a creative department, the final product is a cabinet containing all the hardware and software neeeded to run a game in a casino.
Project's description
Gaming is a millionaire industry in the USA and all over the world. Casinos have moved from the old-style mechanical reel machines to modern video games, sometimes imitating the same cabinet style and game types, but also enhancing player´s experience through the use of different motivators like attractive sounds and animations, second screens, puzzles and skill-based games.


Some of the most relevant project tasks are: designing game ruls and its math; to develop all the necessary software to present the player an attractive game; providing to the operators a configurable and auditable platform; conducting research regarding new technologies; to develop hardware devices and drivers; developing accounting and administration tools for the backend; and designing and programing auditing tools.
Project´s scope
Platform
The game platform is a modular architecture that has proven to be maintainable and adaptable over time and changes. Games are developed in C++ providing an efficient programming language and close to the hardware when needed. Graphics and dynamics are described in a proprietary scripting language and rendered by means of a custom multimedia engine. The software must run unattained for long periods (up to weeks) so stability is as important as performance. The games and their hardware are compliant to most strict standards such as US Nevada state regulations.

Math
Games of chance behave strictly based on random outcomes, yet game rules and winning combinations are designed in order to give the casino an edge on the long run. Rules, probabilities, combinations and awards are statically computed and also proven by simulations. Different figures like Payout percentage, Drop Hold, game Volatility, Coin Out Distribution, etc. are studied,in that they constitute a part of the game profile as important to the player as the graphics and sounds.

Hardware
Electronic Gaming Machines are enclosed in a cabinet, as attractive as secure and cheat-proof. Several peripherals like currency acceptors, coin validators, barcode printers, are used as a mean of interaction with the player. In some cases, like button panels, wire harness and interface boards the hardware are prototyped in-house to be then replicated in mass production facilities.

Backend
Casinos relies on games and backend tools to perform accounting, monitor games and player activity, player tracking and loyalty rewards. Games have their internal accounting tools, and they are monitored by hosts which group the accounting, handle common progressive prizes, maintain databases of valid vouchers, etc. Games are also able to communicate to third party back ends using standard industry protocols.
