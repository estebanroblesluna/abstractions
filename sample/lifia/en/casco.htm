Short bio
I graduated in 1999 as a Licentiate in Computer Science (M.Sc. equivalent) at Universidad Nacional de La Plata (UNLP), in Argentina. In July 2005, I got a PhD. from the FernUnivesität Hagen, in Germany. In 1993, I joined LIFIA, a research laboratory at UNLP, in Argentina. From February 2000 until February 2004, I worked as a researcher at the CONCERT division at Fraunhofer-ISPI, in Germany. Since February 2004, I am back at LIFIA where I manage Knowledge Transfer Partnerships, with special focus on R&D and corporate training initiatives. Since 1994, I am involved in teaching activities for the undergraduate and graduate programs at UNLP, Universidad Nacional de Lujan, and Universidad Abierta Interamericana.
Research interest
My research interests are in various topics of CSCW and Object Oriented Software Engineering. In particular, I am interested in groupware tailorability and in the identification and separation of crosscutting concerns in groupware. In my doctoral thesis I tackled the problem of the lack of computer support for distributed team members that need to perform tailoring in the context of teamwork. The challenge of tailoring in the context of teamwork is to understand and support the needs of the group members, from the moment they encounter a breakdown during work until they have enacted the changes to groupware they deem necessary. My current work approaches the identification and separation of concerns in groupware, e.g., by the application of aspect oriented programming, as a means to reach better tailorability.
